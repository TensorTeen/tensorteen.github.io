Excited to share that I'll be presenting my research, **"Towards Sustainable Scientific Machine Learning: Fast and Interpretable PDE Solvers via RBF-PIELM"** at the ACM India Joint International Conference on Data Science (hashtag#cods 2025) !

In the domain of AI for Science, we need PDE solvers that are not just accurate, but also fast and sustainable. Our work introduces Radial Basis Function-based Physics-Informed Extreme Learning Machines (RBF-PIELM), a lightweight, mesh-free alternative to traditional Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs).

### Key Contributions:

- 𝗠𝗮𝘀𝘀𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗘𝗳𝗳𝗶𝗰𝗶𝗲𝗻𝗰𝘆 𝗚𝗮𝗶𝗻𝘀: RBF-PIELM achieves orders-of-magnitude reduction in runtime and energy usage by replacing PINNs' time-consuming gradient descent with a single-shot least-squares solve.
- 𝗦𝘂𝘀𝘁𝗮𝗶𝗻𝗮𝗯𝗶𝗹𝗶𝘁𝘆: Using the Green Algorithms Methodology, RBF-PIELM requires significantly less energy and produces substantially lower carbon emissions compared to PINNs. This is a major step towards sustainable scientific computing.
- 𝗦𝘂𝗽𝗲𝗿𝗶𝗼𝗿 𝗣𝗲𝗿𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗲: For comparable solution accuracy, RBF-PIELM trains up to 350x faster and requires up to 13x fewer parameters than PINNs.
- 𝗜𝗻𝘁𝗲𝗿𝗽𝗿𝗲𝘁𝗮𝗯𝗶𝗹𝗶𝘁𝘆: The approach offers greater interpretability through physics-aware kernel initialization.

This research lowers the hardware barrier for AI in science, enabling broader accessibility for researchers with limited compute resources.
